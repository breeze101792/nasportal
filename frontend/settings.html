IP Translation

When the portal is accessed from one network, some apps may only be reachable from another (e.g. the same NAS shows up as 192.168.1.10 on Wi-Fi and 10.147.x.x over a tunnel). Map a known IP to its equivalent on the other side — the resolver will use the translated IP whenever the visitor is on the destination network.

Heads up: translation is single-level only. A chain like A→B, B→C is not followed — only the first hop is applied. Keep entries short and direct.

Network Scan

Probe a local network for HTTP services so you don't have to add them by hand. The scan runs from your browser, not the server — it sees what your machine sees, and nothing about the server's network is exposed. Each port is tried as http:// first and falls back to https://, so HTTPS-only services are detected too.

Single IP, CIDR, or IP range.
Comma- or space-separated. Single ports (80) and ranges (5000-6000) can be mixed. Up to 1024 unique ports per scan; total candidates (hosts × ports) capped at 4096.
"Both" is the default and catches services that listen on either port. Pick one when you only care about a specific scheme (e.g. an https-only dashboard).
